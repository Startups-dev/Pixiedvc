# Renting DVC Points vs Buying Disney Vacation Club

## Introduction
Many guests who enjoy renting DVC points eventually wonder whether buying Disney Vacation Club makes sense. Both options can be good choices, depending on how often you travel and how much flexibility you want.

This guide explains the differences clearly so you can decide what fits your lifestyle.

## What Buying Disney Vacation Club Means
Buying DVC means purchasing a long term vacation ownership interest. Owners receive a set number of points each year and pay annual dues.

Ownership works best for families who travel to Disney regularly and plan to return for many years.

## What Renting DVC Points Means
Renting allows guests to enjoy DVC resorts without the long term commitment. You pay only for the stay you want and do not pay annual dues.

This option offers flexibility and lower upfront cost.

## Cost Comparison Over Time
Buying DVC requires a significant upfront investment, plus ongoing dues.

Renting spreads costs over time and avoids long term obligations. For occasional visitors, renting is often more economical.

## Flexibility and Commitment
Ownership provides booking priority and long term access but reduces flexibility.

Renting offers freedom to choose when and if you return.

## Who Each Option Is Best For
Buying may be a good fit for:

1. Families who visit Disney every year
2. Guests committed to long term Disney travel
3. Planners comfortable with upfront investment

Renting may be a better fit for:

1. First time DVC guests
2. Occasional Disney visitors
3. Travelers who value flexibility

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can renters become owners later
Yes. Many owners first experience DVC by renting.

### Does renting help decide if buying is worth it
Yes. Renting allows guests to experience DVC without commitment.
